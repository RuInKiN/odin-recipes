Lasaagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich layers of pasta, savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. Each layer is carefully assembled to create a hearty and comforting meal that is both satisfying and flavorful. The combination of tender pasta sheets, robust tomato sauce, and a blend of cheeses makes lasagna a favorite for family gatherings and special occasions.

Traditionally, lasagna is baked until the top is golden and bubbly, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully. Variations of lasagna can include different meats, vegetables, or even seafood, making it a versatile dish that can be adapted to suit various tastes and dietary preferences. Its delicious aroma and comforting texture have made lasagna a beloved staple in kitchens around the world.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Italian sausage
  • Onion
  • garlic
  • Tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions; drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic, sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add Italian sausage to the skillet and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
  5. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, and crushed tomatoes. Add salt, pepper, and Italian herbs. Simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg and a bit of salt and pepper.
  7. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce. Layer noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella, and more sauce. Repeat layers, finishing with sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 15–20 minutes, until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  9. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
